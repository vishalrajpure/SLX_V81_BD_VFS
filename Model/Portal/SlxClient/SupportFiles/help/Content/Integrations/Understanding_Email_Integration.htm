Understanding Email Integration

The e-mail integration features provide the ability to send e-mail messages from Saleslogix using Outlook or to send e-mail messages from Outlook to one or more Saleslogix contacts or leads and record them as Saleslogix history items.

E-mail Integration

  • Send SLX button adds a history record in the Saleslogix Client.
  • Save e-mail attachments to Saleslogix.
  • Records to Saleslogix History from your inbox or sent items.
  • Drag and drop e-mail messages to the Saleslogix History tab.
  • Use the Saleslogix Address book.
  • Attach Saleslogix Library documents to e-mail messages.
  • Attach a Saleslogix Contact vCard in e-mail messages.
  • You can also send a Saleslogix contact’s information in vCard format from Outlook.
  • Include attachments from the Attachments tab by dragging them into the message.

Important Information about E-mail Integration

When you use the e-mail integration features, keep the following in mind:

  • Typing contacts’ names: If you type part or all of a contact’s name in the To box, and more than one contact is found, the first found contact’s e-mail address is used. In addition, if your company uses Oracle as the database platform, you must type uppercase/lowercase letters accurately to locate a contact's name.
  • Recording Notes/History from a message you are sending: The Send SLX button appears on each e-mail message you compose in Outlook. Click this button to send your message and record notes/history information. The message is recorded to the Notes/History tab for each Saleslogix contact or lead and any associated account. Saleslogix locates the contact or lead based on a matching e-mail address. For example, if you compose a message in Outlook to jsmith@smithco.com and click Send SLX, Saleslogix looks for a contact or lead containing the same e-mail address, and if it is found, records the message to notes/history. If a matching e-mail address is not found, a dialog box appears stating that the contact was not found and no notes/history will be recorded. (However, you can record the notes/history by dragging the message from Outlook to the Saleslogix Client.) If more than one contact is found, you are prompted to choose a contact to receive the notes/history information.
    If the message includes attachment(s), you are prompted to keep a copy of the attachment(s) in Saleslogix. If you click Yes, a link to the attachment is added to the Attachments tab for each contact or lead.
  • Recording Notes/History from a message you receive: The Record to History button appears in the main Outlook window. Select a message and click Record to History (on the Tools menu), or drag the message from Outlook to the Saleslogix contact record. Attachments are recorded as described previously for the Send SLX button.
  • Excluding contacts from your organization: Your system administrator can set up domain name exclusion rules to prevent notes/history records from being recorded for some contacts (such as employees at your company). The same rules apply when messages are automatically flagged for follow up. For more information, consult your system administrator or refer to the Administrator Help topic "Adding a Domain Exclusion".
  • Sending messages without a connection to the Exchange server: The Saleslogix Address Book in Outlook is not dependent on a connection to the host Exchange server, but rather on the connection to the database (Network or Remote). When you click the Send SLX button, the message is recorded to notes/history in Saleslogix; however, the message remains in your Outlook Outbox until a connection is made to the Exchange server.
  • Sending messages with Saleslogix closed: If the Saleslogix Windows Client is closed when you click the Send SLX or Record to History button, a temp file is written to a temporary directory. When you open the Saleslogix Client, the notes/history information is recorded.
